Yellowstone National Park

  • Home to the largest concentration of geysers in the world, with over 500 geysers, including the famous Old Faithful, which erupts approximately every 90 minutes.
  • The park boasts a supervolcano known as the Yellowstone Caldera. While dormant, it is responsible for the geothermal activity and features found throughout the park.
  • Yellowstone is a wildlife haven, with species such as grizzly bears, gray wolves, bison, elk, and pronghorn roaming its vast wilderness.
  • The park's Grand Canyon showcases stunning waterfalls, including the iconic Lower Falls, which is nearly twice as tall as Niagara Falls.

Serengeti National Park:

  • The Serengeti is renowned for its annual wildebeest migration, where millions of wildebeest, zebras, and other herbivores travel across the plains in search of fresh grazing.
  • It is home to one of the highest concentrations of large predators in Africa, including lions, cheetahs, and leopards.
  • The park is a birdwatcher's paradise, with over 500 bird species, including ostriches, eagles, and storks.
  • Serengeti's vast landscapes include diverse ecosystems, from open grasslands (known as savannahs) to riverine forests and granite outcrops called kopjes.

Banff National Park

  • Banff is Canada's oldest national park, established in 1885, and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • The park features stunning turquoise lakes, such as Lake Louise and Moraine Lake, known for their mesmerizing colors caused by glacial silt.
  • Banff is home to a variety of wildlife, including black bears, grizzly bears, moose, mountain goats, and bighorn sheep.
  • The Icefields Parkway, a scenic highway passing through the park, offers breathtaking views of glaciers, towering mountains, and cascading waterfalls.
